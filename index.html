Visualizing SEP

This website provides a visual search interface to the corpus of articles in the Stanford Encylopedia of Philosophy. Using network mapping, this site shows how each article in the SEP is linked to every other article.

  • Each entry in the search menu is the title of an article from the SEP.
  • When you select an article from the search menu, an interactive graph will be generated showing how that article is linked to other related articles on the site.
    • A preview of the article
  • The search menu defaults to showing articles about philosophers, but it can be filtered dynamically to show either philosophers or ideas.
    • The graph generated will always show articles about both philosophers and ideas
      (assuming the respective article links to both kinds).
    • Nodes colored in blue are articles about philosophers, while nodes colored in orange are articles about ideas.
  • Each node is an active link, so you can explore the related articles by double-clicking on the node.
