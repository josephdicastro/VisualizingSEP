Article Graph Instructions

The Article Graph selects a single SEP article, and then shows every other article in the entire Encyclopedia that the selection is linked to in some way. The selected article is always at the center of the graph, and the related articles spread radially around it.

Navigate the Graph

Interaction Methods

  • Hover/MouseOver
    • When you hover over a node or title, the left sidebar will be updated with details from that node. "(Preview)" will be appended to the end of the article name in the sidebar.
    • When the graph is in "Hover Mode", the graph itself will be redrawn with information about how the previewed node relates to other articles from the main selection.
  • Click
    • If you click a node or a title, the graph will be frozen, so you can then explore the relationships among the linked articles.
  • Double Click
    • Double-Clicking any node or title will reload the Article Graph will the double-clicked node as the central node.

Hover Mode and Click Mode

  • There are two fundamental ways to navigate the graph: Hover Mode and Click Mode. These are exclusive modes, and the graph can only be in one mode at a time.
  • The current mode is always listed in the “Graph Mode” box in the top right corner. Toggle between the two modes by single-clicking this box. Switching modes will clear out the graph.
  • Regardless of the current Graph Mode, Double-click any node or title to make a new selection and reload the graph with the new selection.

Preview Mode

  • This is the default modal state of the graph.
  • In this mode, when you hover/mouseOver over a node or title, the entire graph will be re-drawn to preview the information that emerges from the hovered reference. The article details in the left sidebar will be temporarily replaced with preview information for the hovered node, and if the hovered node also links to other articles in the same graph, a dashed line will be drawn between the nodes that are also linked together to indicate this relationship.
  • As soon as you stop hovering over that node, the graph will return to its original state. And when you hover over another node, the graph will be redrawn, etc.
  • At any point, you can always double-click a node or title to draw a brand new graph with this new node as the main selection.
  • Preview Mode provides a powerful way to quickly view the relationships in the Article Graph. However, it doesn’t really allow a deep exploration of the relationships between the selected article and the links.

Explore Mode

  • Explore Mode is for deep-diving into the relationships between the selected entry and the linked articles, as well as to make a more detailed inquiry into the linked articles themselves.
  • The power of this mode comes in when the user single-clicks a node or title. The graph will then be frozen in that state. Each time the users clicks a new node, the graph updates and is then re-frozen.
  • In this state, the user can then explore all the details of all the linked articles, can follow how linked articles are themselves linked to other articles in the graph, or can look at, for examplke, only articles that are out-going links or only articles whose primary domain is epistemology, etc.
  • When you hover over a node or title in Explore Mode, the data in the left sidebar will be replaced with a preview of the hovered article, but the main graph features remain the same. The graph itself is only re-drawn when you click a node.
  • At any point, you can always double-click a node or title to draw a brand new graph with this new node as the main selection.

Color

Each article node and title is colored according to its primary domain designation, which is the domain chosen as the most representative expression of the article’s topic type. In many cases though, there are fluid boundaries between domain categories and many, perhaps most, articles are tagged as appearing in multiple domains. The color of the node and title will always flow from the article’s primary domain.

Left Sidebar (Same as Domain Graph)

The left side bar contains details about a selected article. In it’s default state, the left side bar provides:

  • A preview of (up to) the first 500 characters in the full SEP article.
  • A link to read the full article on the SEP website.
  • A panel called "Explore the TOC" that is collapsed by default.
  • The Domain designation(s) of the selected article.
  • Details about the author(s), publication date(s), and word count.

“Explore the TOC”

  • By default, the “Explore the TOC” panel is collapsed.
  • Single-click on the panel heading, or toggle button, to expand its contents.
  • This panel contains the complete outline from the full SEP article’s Table of Contents. Each TOC heading is an active link, so clicking on a heading will link you into that particular section of the article on the SEP website.
  • When you expand the “Explore the TOC” panel, the Domain(s) and the Article Details panels will collapse. Single-clicking the “Explore the TOC” heading again will then re-collapse this panel, and restore the Domain and Article Details panels.

Right Sidebar

The right side bar contains details about the linked articles. In its default state, the right sidebar provides:

  • Link Count. This shows the number of links that connect to the main selection.
  • List of Articles. This is collapsed by default.
  • Link Domains: A list of the primary domain designations for each of the linked articles, as well as a count of how many articles in that domain.
  • Link Directions: A list of the three kinds of link directions: bi-directional links, incoming links, and outgoing links; and a count of how many linked articles fall into each direction catergory.

“List of Articles”

  • By default, the “List of Articles” panel is collapsed.
  • Single-click on the panel heading, or its toggle button, to expand its contents.
  • This panel contains an alphabetical list of all the linked articles in the radial graph, each title also colored by its primary domain designation.
  • Because this information duplicates the nodes and titles in the radial graph, it is hidden by default. But some graphs are so large that having the alphabetical list available is helpful for exploration.
  • When you expand the “List of Articles” panel, the Link Domains and Link Directions panels will collapse. Single-clicking the “List of Articles” heading again will then collapse this panel, and restore the Link Domains and Link Directions panels

Recent Searches (Same as Domain Graph)

  • The Recent Searches drop-down menu list the the 10 most recent searches on the site, inclusive of both the Article Graph and the Domain Graph. New searches are added/removed on a first-in/first-out basis.

Domain Graph Instructions

The Domain Graph shows how articles in a specific domain are all linked together. An individual article may appear in multiple domains, but it will always be colored by its primary domain designation. When the graph is first loaded, the entire domain network is displayed, but the labels are hidden.

The Domain Graph differs from the Article Graph in that the Article Graph shows every link a particular SEP article has, while the Domain Graph will only show articles that are tagged as being members of the same domain.

Right Sidebar

The right side bar lists all of the articles in the selected domain. It has three major sections:

  • Count of Domain Articles.
  • Most Connected Nodes. This panel lists the top five most connected articles in the domain, sorted in descending order by the total number of connections within this domain. As usual: each title is colored by its primary domain designation. The number preceeding the title is the number of domain connections for that particular article.
  • List of All Domain Articles. This is an alphabetic list of every single article in the domain, where each title is colored by its primary domain designation. The number preceeding the title is the number of domain connections for that particular article.
